The Problem

Context

Worldwide, people with disabilities face accessibility barriers on the internet due to inaccessible websites, applications, and digital content. These barriers can limit access to education, employment, communication, and information. Lack of awareness, inadequate accessibility standards, and unequal access to technology contribute to digital exclusion, making internet accessibility an important issue for social inclusion and equal opportunities.

Who Is Affected

  • People with visual impairments (blindness, low vision)
  • People with hearing impairments
  • People with motor disabilities (limited hand/arm mobility)
  • People with cognitive or learning disabilities
  • Elderly users who often share similar accessibility needs

Why the Problem Matters

Most modern websites lack proper semantic tags, screen reader support, and high-contrast options. This prevents millions of people from accessing education, employment, and essential public services online.

Proposed Solutions & Research

Actionable Steps

  1. Designing websites according to accessibility standards (WCAG).
  2. Providing captions, transcripts, and alternative text for all digital content.
  3. Educating developers and content creators about digital inclusion and accessibility.

Logbook Entry #1

What problem did I choose and why?

We chose accessibility barriers for people with disabilities because it is an important social issue that affects millions of people. Accessibility helps ensure equal opportunities and social inclusion.

What contexts or data influenced my choice?

We considered how many people encounter difficulties accessing buildings, transportation systems, websites, and online services. Accessibility is increasingly important in a digital society.

What are the possible solutions I identified?

  • Improve accessible web design.
  • Increase accessibility in public spaces.
  • Develop awareness and training programs.

What questions or doubts do I have?

  • How can accessibility standards be implemented more effectively?
  • What technologies can help people with disabilities?
  • How can governments and organizations promote inclusive design?
